Project Description and Motivation


The goal of our project is the creation of M.A.R.S. which is short for
"Magnetic ATP Recycling System". It is a bioreactor capable of cyclic ATP regeneration by use of light.

"So, you want to create ATP. But what is ATP and why is it so important?"

A lot of chemical reactions would not be possible without enzymes catalyzing them. However, also those enzymes often do not work without the help of certain molecules known as cofactors. A very important cofactor is the Adenosintriphosphate (ATP). Its highly energetic Phosphate bonds provide the necessary driving force for enzymatic conversion reactions inside the cell. This is the reason for ATP to often be referred to as the energy currency of cells. It is a necessary component of all life.

"Great! Then let's create as much ATP as possible! Or is there a problem?"

Yes, basically the more ATP the better! But the energy carried by an ATP molecule must be invested during its regeneration from Adenosindiphosphate (ADP) and Phosphate. In living organisms this creation takes place in the course of cell respiration which is a very complex process that cannot be emulated artificially. Thus, ATP needed in industrial processes and science has to be created differently. Currently used methods consume a lot of resources and the chemical synthesis causes avoidable pollution. So, industrial production of ATP is expensive and environmentally harmful so far.

"And what can be done to improve the current situation?"

This is the point where our project M.A.R.S. enters the stage. We want to improve the current situation by developing a bioreactor capable of cyclic ATP creation. The energy needed for this is taken from sunlight and all resources involved are reused in the next cycle so that only the basic components of ATP, ADP and Phosphate, need to be provided again. This makes ATP production with M.A.R.S. environmentally friendly and much cheaper than previous methods.Â 

"How do you want to achieve your goal?"

We use a previously found approach for ATP regeneration where Liposomes are equipped with Bacteriorhodopsin and ATP-Synthase. Together those membrane proteins are able to store the energy of sunlight as chemical energy in the form of ATP. In M.A.R.S. this ATP regeneration step is followed by magnetic immobilization of the chassis carrying Bacteriorhodopsin and ATP-Synthase. Those chassis are Liposomes in the original approach. However, in M.A.R.S. we use Polymersomes. Immobilizing them after the ATP regeneration step enables harvesting the ATP while the chassis remain functioning. As the immobilization is induced by a magnetic field it can be reversed by turning off this field. So, turning off the magnetic field enables another cycle of ATP regeneration.

As you can imagine, we would not be able to create M.A.R.S. without hard-working allies. In the following they want to introduce themselves in more detail:

I am Bacteriorhodopsin and I love to build up proton gradients on membranes. However, to perform this task I need energy in the form of light. Originally, I come from Halobacterium salinarium where I work together with my colleague ATP-Sythase. By transforming light into chemical energy, we help H. salinarium to survive under very salty conditions.

I am ATP-Synthase. As my colleague Bacteriorhodopsin already told you, I help in the process of transforming light to chemical energy in H. salinarium. After Bacteriorhodopsin has built up the proton gradient by use of light I, in turn, use the proton gradient to covalently bind ADP and Phosphate to ATP. However, although we originally work together in H. salinarium the version of me that is found in M.A.R.S. is isolated from Sacharomyces cerevisiae.

I am a Polymersome. Many of my kind can be found in the bioreactor M.A.R.S. created by iGEM Aachen. We serve as basic chassis carrying Bacteriorhodopsin and ATP-Synthase around which enables their work. We are made of a PETOz-PDMS-PETOz triblock polymer, 100-500nm large in diameter and resemble the structure of lipid vesicles. However, we are preferred over Liposomes as we are more stable. So, M.A.R.S. is more reliable with chassis of my kind.

I am one of many magnetic Polystyrene beads. Our task in M.A.R.S. is to enable the magnetic separation of the chassis which makes their reuse in following cycles possible. Each of us is bound to multiple Polymersomes because with 3.7μm in diameter we are much larger then them.

We are an Anchor Peptide, a Peptide Spacer and a Polystyrene Binding Protein forming one complex. Thus, we also want to introduce ourselves together. The three of us connect one Polymersome with a Polystyrene Bead whereby I, the Anchor Peptide bind to the Polymersome, I, the Polystyrene Binding Protein bind to the Polystyrene bead and I, the Peptide Spacer connect the two others.
