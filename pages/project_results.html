Project Results

Success does not come instantly, it is gained drop by drop



During the competition we as a team worked on several very different sides of our project. We went even further, outside the laboratory, to create models and hardware for M.A.R.S. and took our project to the new levels by creating meaningful discourse with experts, companies and people of every imaginable background, to communicate science.

In the end, all of our efforts and activities came together and created a complete picture. Here, we dissect all of the different sides of our project and present to you our main results on each of them.
Use the Pipette by clicking on it to see our main project results.
Lab Results
  • Successful liposome formation
  • Successful identification of magnetic particle binding peptide
  • Binding tests on liposomes with different candidate peptides
  • Implementation testing in a real industrial context at FZ Jülich
HP & IHP
  • Several very insightful discussions with experts on topics related to M.A.R.S.
  • Detailed reflection of the impact and responsibilities of M.A.R.S. and us as a team
  • Communication with people and companies outside of synthetic biology or science in general
Hardware
  • Design of a mostly 3D-printable bioreactor
  • Modeling of the complete bioreactor as well as single components
  • Start of production of parts of the reactors shaking mechanism
  • Coded the server side communication protocol for an IoT platform
Modeling
  • Successful molecular dynamics simulation of all components of our system
  • Extensive mathematical modelling
  • Parameter optimizations for M.A.R.S. application at FZ Jülich
  • Creation of visualizations of the molecular mechanisms of M.A.R.S.
Science Communication
  • Creation of a network of different projects connected to our educational framework “Science for everyone”
  • Intensive discussions about science, our project, the chances and responsibilities of synthetic biology
  • Communication and exchange between people, from various backgrounds. Newcomers, prospective and established scientists, as well as the broad public
